Mapping Perspectives: Constructivism Investigation





We see a couple picnicking in the grass. Then we realize they are not just sitting in the grass, but in a large park, adjacent to a harbor, in a large city built right on the ocean, or rather on a very large lake, Lake Michigan, which itself is surrounded by even larger land masses. It follows the earth, the solar system, our galaxy, and finally the whole universe, surrounded by a square on whose side: '100 million light years' and on the other side: '10^24m' is written, and is supposed to represent the largest possible size of the universe. After that the video 'zooms in' again, from 10^24 meters to 10^-13 meters, the size of an atomic nucleus, which itself consists of further particles. The described scenario from the video Powers of Ten by Eames Office allows the following interpretation: depending on the scaling and the viewing plane, reality looks different. Not only in the scaling direction upwards, but also downwards, objects seem to be homogeneous and flat, but this is presented as a kind of optical illusion by zooming in and out in the video. Further, a possible interpretation of the video could be that the viewing and scaling levels determine what we see, or, to put it bluntly, how reality is constituted.

The project addressed the question whether and to what extent the adoption of specific viewing perspectives is related to the construction of the object of observation. The observation was split into three perspectives of three different levels: Micro (perspectives of individuals), Meso (perspectives of cultural groups) and Macro (perspectives of nature). In particular, the aim is to contrast between different perspectives and to illustrate the extent to which the same object, viewed from different perspectives, is structured differently or has different definitional boundaries.
